Chapter 0 : Preliminary Ideas#

Introduction#

Quantum Computing is one of most famous terms in modern era. If someone is a little bit interested in computational field, I am sure they have came across this term. But what is Quantum Computing? Quantum Computing is a type of computation where we use the power of quantum mechanics (eg: use ideas such as superposition, interference, entanglement etc). Devices that perform quantum computations are known as Quantum Computers.

But why do we need these type of devices?, The classical or modern computers are doing just fine. These devices can actually simulate atomic structures, planetary motions, fluid dy- namics and many more things. The main reason is that Quantum Computers have the potential to revolutionize computation by making certain types of classically intractable problems solvable.

The best example of this is the Shor’s Algorithm which let us factorise a number \(N\) with a complexity of \(O(\log(N))\). For Classical Computers, it is not possible, even in dreams.

Another such example is Grover’s Algorithm. This is a search algorithm for unsorted list, with a complexity of \(\sqrt{O(N)}\), where in classical computers the complexity is \(O(N)\). This is not all. Due to the nature of Hilbert Space and classical bits, the Classical Computers need much more time and memeory to simulate time evolutions of quantum systems, but the same thing can be done very easily in quantum computers.

As, discussed there are so many advantages of Quantum Com- puters over classical ones (in some cases). But how the very idea came into the existence?
